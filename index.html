    University Work

    Dye Sensitized Solar Cells

    Final Year Project

    For my final year project at university I fabricated and tested the efficiency of Dye Sensitized Solar Cells (DSSC) based on Natural Dyes. This involved characterising the efficiency of a silicon solar cell in a simulated solar rig before fabricating and testing the completed cells. The project largely revolved around experimenting with dyes extracted from berries and leaves and combining them to take advantage of their improved light absorbance at different wavelengths.

    Computing

    Computational Physics Unit

    As part of my computational physics unit I created an Earth-Moon rocket orbit simulation. This was done to show the solving of the 2nd order differential equations for a rocket orbiting a fixed mass. The exercise started with creating a simulation of a rocket orbiting the Earth and then developed further to include a trip around the Moon.
